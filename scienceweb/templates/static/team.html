David Alcántara
(President, COO and co-founder. MBA)



David Alcántara, PhD, MBA. is currently the President and founder of the Society for the Improvement of Science (SACSIS). Co-founder and Chief Operations Officer at Peerland, he also serves as the Editor in Chief at The All Results Journals since 2008. At present, Dr. Alcantara is Senior Marie Curie fellow at the Andalusian Center for Nanomedicine (Bionand, Spain). He previously worked as Fulbright and Marie Curie fellow at Harvard Medical School with Prof. Lee Josephson developing a new sensing nanotechnology platform for biomedical applications. Dr. Alcantara is an active scientific entrepreneur with experience in the third and editorial sectors.


Emilio González
(BDM and co-founder. IE business school MFSR)



Emilio González, PhD. BDM of Peerland. Emilio is an experienced entrepreneur formed in the Silicon Valley and at the IE business school of Madrid. He previously was a postdoctoral researcher at Christopher Contag lab within the Molecular Imaging Program at Stanford (MIPS) and the biotech company TransdermInc working on RNAi therapeutics and delivery of siRNAs to skin for the treatment of the rare skin disorder Pachyonychia congenita. While at Stanford he actively participated in AIMS (the postdoc link to entrepreneurship at Stanford) and co-founded the biotech Skin Deep Diagnostics.


Enrique Pelaez
(Engineer)



Enrique Peláez - Enrique is the Head Software Engineer at Peerland. He has a degree in Computer Engineering, Information Technology and Software Engineering obtained at Malaga University. Throughout his work he has specialized in multi-platform systems administration, virtualization systems, embedded system design and multilingual programming. From November 2011 is IT Manager at the Andalusian Center of Nanomedicine and Biotechnology (BIONAND).

Mª Belén Suárez
(Community Manager)



Mª Belén Su´rez, PhD. is currently the Community Manager of the Society for the Improvement of Science (SACSIS) and The All Results Journals creating, managing and growing their presence through blogs, Facebook, and other strategically relevant online properties. Her experience in Quality Management Systems in the private sector enables her to manage project's documentation, as well as setting goals and indicators or supplier management. She previously worked in the Laboratory of Bioorganic Synthesis, at the University Louis Pasteur, directed by Dr. Charles Mioskowski and Dr. Alain Wagner in the synthesis of compounds derived from phosphorus used as target molecular templates for asymmetric catalysis and molecular recognition. During her PhD she worked developing new approximations for the asymmetric synthesis of chiral compounds with added values.
