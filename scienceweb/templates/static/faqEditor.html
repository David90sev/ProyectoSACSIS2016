FAQs

Why should we pay the reviewers?

    The peer review system has been based on the "today you, tomorrow me"" system. However, great reviewers are a valuable scarcity in the current trends. Besides, some revisions have turned out to be of bad quality, incomplete or delayed, increasing costs and affecting the reputation of journals. We think the reviewer's job (experts who normally give great reviews) should operate in the same way as the evaluator's job already is: economically rewarded and at the same time qualified (or recognized). We believe in the reviewer's honesty and reputation. We also think that with our system, paying the reviewers would not cause an incremental price increase for journal acceptance as we provide services that save costs to the edition process. By paying the reviewers you will have a professional job completed by professionals of the reviewing process (besides being solid experts in their fields).

What are my duties?

    As the core part of the peer review system the editor would need to participate in the following activities:

  • Editors have the last word on choosing the reviewers.
  • You will have free access to reviewer's profiles.
  • Editors can open an account in Peerland to cover the cost of peer review.
  • You will need to have enough cash in your account to pay for the work you're immediately requesting from a reviewer so Peerland can pay the reviewer or donate to the corresponding organization.
  • Editors need to evaluate reviewers after their work.
  • Once you have received the revision from a reviewer you will have five days to communicate your decision to accept the revision and evaluate the reviewer. Then Peerland will pay the reviewer.
  • You will need to upload the instructions for reviewers and other necessary documentation specific to carry out the review process in your journal.
